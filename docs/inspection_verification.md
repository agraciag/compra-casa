# Property Inspection and Verification Processes

## Overview
Thorough inspection and verification of properties is critical to avoid costly surprises after purchase. This document outlines the essential steps for verifying property condition, legal status, and compliance with regulations.

## Pre-Purchase Verification Checklist

### 1. Legal Documentation Verification
- **Title Deed (Escritura de Propiedad)**: Verify ownership and legal status
- **Land Registry Certificate (Certificado del Registro de la Propiedad)**: Check for liens, mortgages, or encumbrances
- **Urban Planning Compliance (Licencia de Primera Ocupación)**: Ensure property complies with zoning regulations
- **Community Documentation**: Review HOA rules, fees, and financial status
- **Energy Certificate (Certificado Energético)**: Verify energy efficiency rating
- **IBI Receipts**: Confirm property tax payments are up to date

### 2. Physical Inspection Requirements

#### Basic Visual Inspection
- Structural integrity (walls, ceilings, floors)
- Signs of moisture or water damage
- Electrical system condition
- Plumbing and water pressure
- Heating and cooling systems
- Windows and doors (seals, locks, functionality)
- Roof condition (for houses with private roof access)

#### Detailed Technical Inspection
- Professional structural engineer assessment
- Electrical installation safety check
- Plumbing system evaluation
- HVAC system performance
- Insulation quality assessment
- Asbestos or hazardous material check (for older buildings)
- Accessibility compliance (if needed)

### 3. Utilities and Services Verification
- Water supply and quality
- Electrical connection capacity
- Gas installation safety
- Internet and telecommunications infrastructure
- Waste disposal systems
- Building maintenance systems

## Professional Inspections

### 4. Certified Building Inspection
- **Purpose**: Comprehensive assessment of structural integrity
- **Professional**: Qualified architect or technical architect
- **Cost**: €400-800 for standard apartment
- **Deliverable**: Detailed report with photos and recommendations
- **Timeline**: 1-2 weeks for report delivery

### 5. Specialized Inspections
- **Electrical Safety**: Certified electrician inspection
- **Gas Safety**: Gas installation verification
- **Plumbing**: Pressure tests and leak detection
- **Pest Control**: Termite or other pest inspection
- **Environmental**: Radon, asbestos, or other environmental hazards

## Verification of Property Boundaries and Dimensions

### 6. Cadastral Verification
- Compare cadastral records with actual property
- Verify declared surface area matches reality
- Check for discrepancies in property registry
- Confirm outdoor spaces (terraces, gardens) are legally recognized

### 7. Boundary Confirmation
- Verify property boundaries with neighbors
- Check for encroachments or boundary disputes
- Confirm access rights and easements
- Validate shared space agreements

## Community and Building Verification

### 8. Building Condition Assessment
- Common areas condition
- Elevator maintenance records
- Building insurance coverage
- Reserve fund status
- Planned renovation projects
- Noise levels and acoustic insulation

### 9. Community Financial Health
- Review community financial statements
- Check for outstanding debts
- Verify insurance coverage adequacy
- Understand upcoming expenses
- Review meeting minutes for potential issues

## Municipal and Regulatory Compliance

### 10. City Hall Verification
- **Habitability Certificate (Cédula de Habitabilidad)**: Confirm property is legally habitable
- **Planning Permission**: Verify any renovations were properly permitted
- **Municipal Charges**: Check for unpaid municipal fees
- **Building License**: For houses, verify original construction was permitted

### 11. Utility Connection Verification
- Legal water, electricity, and gas connections
- Proper waste disposal arrangements
- Compliance with municipal regulations
- Shared utility arrangements (if applicable)

## Environmental Considerations

### 12. Location Risk Assessment
- Flood risk evaluation
- Seismic activity considerations
- Industrial contamination risks
- Airport noise impact
- Traffic and air pollution exposure

## Verification Timeline

### 13. Pre-Offer Stage
- Basic documentation review
- Preliminary visual inspection
- Cadastral verification
- Community fee confirmation

### 14. Post-Offer Stage
- Detailed legal verification
- Professional building inspection
- Specialized system inspections
- Final documentation review

### 15. Pre-Closing Stage
- Final walk-through inspection
- Verification of agreed repairs
- Utility transfer arrangements
- Key handover confirmation

## Red Flags to Watch For

### 16. Legal Issues
- Missing or incomplete documentation
- Discrepancies in property measurements
- Outstanding legal disputes
- Unauthorized renovations
- Expired permits

### 17. Physical Issues
- Significant structural problems
- Major electrical or plumbing defects
- Moisture or mold issues
- Unsafe installations
- Non-compliance with accessibility regulations

## Professional Support Network

### 18. Recommended Professionals
- **Lawyer**: Property law specialist in Aragon region
- **Architect**: For technical inspections
- **Notary**: Local professional familiar with regional requirements
- **Surveyor**: For precise measurements and boundary verification
- **Inspector**: Certified building inspection professional

## Cost-Benefit Analysis

### 19. Inspection Investment vs. Risk Mitigation
- Inspection costs typically 0.2-0.5% of property value
- Potential savings from identifying issues: 5-15% of property value
- Peace of mind value: Priceless

## Post-Inspection Actions

### 20. Issue Resolution
- Negotiate price reduction for identified issues
- Request seller to complete repairs before closing
- Obtain repair cost estimates for future budgeting
- Decide whether to proceed based on findings

## Documentation Requirements

### 21. Records to Maintain
- All inspection reports
- Legal verification documents
- Measurement certificates
- Professional opinions and recommendations
- Correspondence with professionals

Following this comprehensive inspection and verification process will help ensure that your property purchase in Zaragoza is secure, legally sound, and physically suitable for your needs.
